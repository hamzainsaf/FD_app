Informed Consent

You are invited to participate in a cognitive science study investigating how people perceive faces in ambiguous images. The study is conducted by researchers at the University.

What you will do: You will view a series of images and decide whether each contains a face or not, pressing F (Face) or J (No Face). The task takes approximately 20–25 minutes.

Risks: There are no known risks. The task involves only standard computer-based stimuli.

Confidentiality: Your data will be anonymized. No personally identifying information is collected beyond a randomly generated participant ID.

Voluntary participation: Participation is entirely voluntary. You may withdraw at any time without penalty.

Data use: Anonymized data may be used in scientific publications and shared in public repositories.

By clicking "I Agree", you confirm that you have read this information, are 18 years or older, and consent to participate.

Task Instructions

In this experiment, you will see a series of images. Your task is to decide as quickly and accurately as possible whether each image contains a face.

F Face
J No Face
  • Each image appears briefly. Respond before it disappears (3 seconds).
  • The experiment has 4 blocks of 40 trials each (160 trials total).
  • Each block has specific instructions — read them carefully at the start of each block.
  • Some blocks emphasize speed; others emphasize accuracy.
  • Some blocks have more face images; others have fewer.
  • You will start with a 5-trial practice with feedback.
  • The experiment will run in full-screen mode. Please do not exit full screen.
  • Use a keyboard. Touch input is not supported.
⚠ This experiment requires a physical keyboard. Touch devices are not supported.
